UCHealth Nurses are Pioneering Neonatal and Maternal Fetal Care
We understand that sometimes, medical innovations and unsurpassed care come as a result of combining talents. Taking caregivers, clinical teams, innovators and bringing them together to achieve the impossible. At the Colorado institute for Maternal and Fetal Health we do just that. The Denver based institute was formed with the teams from the UCHealth Women's Care Center, Children's Hospital Colorado, and the University of Colorado Medical Campus. This formalized program's entire focus rests on high-risk deliveries and helping to ensure that moms and babies get everything they need in the days leading up to, during and after birth.
Strength in Numbers
Our multi-disciplinary teams have innovated many critical developments in neonatal and maternal fetal care, including:
  • Surfactant replacement for premature babies' lungs
  • Inhaled nitric oxide therapy for pulmonary hypertension, as well as, for the prevention of premature lung disease
  • Brain cooling for perinatal brain injury
  • Optimal nutritional strategies for low birth-weight babies
  • The maternal-fetal group was the first in the region to use 3D/4D ultrasound for evaluation and management of spinal abnormalities
  • The maternal-fetal group was also first to perform stem cell transfer for a genetic blood disorder to a fetus, which was performed at 14 weeks
  • Exit procedures
  • TTTS laser coagulations (SLFP)
  • Intrauterine fetal procedures
  • Deliveries at the location where the baby surgery will take place, keeping mom's and babies together
We help expectant women and families of the Rocky Mountain Region feel supported and safe. As a Clinical RN in the WCC you will partner with clinical teams to deliver the finest evidence based care to your patients and their cherished newborns.
We're looking for a Nurse with the Following:
  • A degree from an ACEN or CCNE accredited school, BSN preferred
  • Active, unrestricted Colorado RN license, or the ability to obtain one
  • Current AHA, BLS and ACLS
  • Current NPR
  • At least one year previous RN experience in high-risk labor and delivery
